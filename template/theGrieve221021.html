The Grieve

October 22, 2021 by eF...eS

"Bob! Bob! Bob!" The sound of basketball bouncing played near by. Sitting under lighting pole; looking at his far left, the temple is shown by big spotlights around it. A low pitch sound of small crowd of women mumbling is carried toward his position by the sea breeze. Behind where he is sitting the dom of McCara garden, under it young racial couple are sitting on one side, on the other side, several gentlemen are having pleasure with their time.



Up higher in the sky, Jupiter is shinning as a crystal. In front of him, lights coming from the ceil of the mosque are drawing his attention to the complex, but gorgeous geometrical shapes and the colorful glasses above its windows and door. Far in front, up and above in the sky, the crescent moon is setting, just behind it on its left, Venus, the second brightest object after the moon is standing out among the faint southern stars in the blue dark night sky.

Amid all these, he recalls the reality that it encapsulates. Indeed, it was a heart-rending moment. He thinks about it after every cycle of blinks. To be told I have no room for you... It's a tough harsh punch on the face. He awes, who is that room for? Why didn't we repel each other from the first spark? Was it because of the mixed signals we transmitted to each other? Ni, I am the one who is desperate for love, companionship and to be pulled out from this lonely grave on earth of seven billion people.


Standing out in the balcony, below outside the opposite house, a crowd of men and women are sitting separately with sad on their faces. Lowering their heads, in shock of the moment, unbelieving what had met them together, are mourning the loss of their beloved one. While a handful men of those whom can with stand the hurtful reality are preparing the coffin of the deceased.



In contrast with his situation, he thinks the different feelings towards him - who is alive and the deceased - who has passed away."Does he even need their accompany? Was he feeling worthy, as he is now, while he was alive? Why does he gets all this attention while he is dead? Why am I standing worthless watching over his burial? Is all this support, love and affection like a golden treasure hidden in a master-safe and only to be revealed when there is no food to eat?" With emotion, all these questions strick his heart at lightening speed.



Should he mourn his lonely soul, like the crowd mourning the lonely body with no soul in the black golden decorated coffin? What do you think?
