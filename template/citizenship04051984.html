Data

Data are meaningless raw facts in the form of texts, numbers, symbols, or emojis. Data can be translated into meaningful information after been processed and analyzed by a computer or human.

In today’s world, we generate an enormous amount of data each day, compared to our ancestors, and it is expected that growth will be exponential in the coming years. According to LinkedIn, on average, 1.7MB of data was created every second by every person during 2020, and 2.5 quintillion bytes were produced by humans every day. Furthermore, in 2018 and 2019 alone, 90% of the world’s data has been created, and 463 exabytes(equivalent to 1 * 1018 Bytes) of data will be generated each day by humans as of 2025. What is more, 95 million photos and videos are shared every day on Instagram, every day306.4 billion emails are sent, 5 million tweets are made, and 44 zettabytes (equivalent to 1 * 1021 Bytes) were the expected amount of data created by the end of 2020.




Citizenship

“Citizenship is a relationship between an individual and a state to which the individual owes allegiance and in turn is entitled to its protection. Each state determines the conditions under which it will recognize persons as its citizens and the conditions under which that status will be withdrawn.” - Wikipedia.

For clarity, citizenship does not necessarily refer to its classical meaning as defined above; rather, it can have a broader sense, any agreement or relationship between an individual and another party in exchange for mutual benefit where that party has the ultimate decision about the fate of the consensus can refer to as citizenship.

Any agreement between an individual and a state, association, or company can count as citizenship.

For illustration, the agreement between a new user and a social media platform, where most new users comply and consent to the prolonged wordy policy of the particular platform without their consciousness. For whatever the excuse, the platform can terminate a user’s account or restrict the use of some features as a temporary punishment. But why should the user endorse the agreement in the first place? If that is the case, how many citizenships are you entitled to? What's more, in exchange for what? Protection, social status, happiness …



Did you know Google users are more than half the population of the world (270 million of those are in the US), Amazon has 300 million active users (that is the entire population of Turkey and Nigeria together), and Facebook has nearly half of the world's population of users? Is it that they do not have a political border on which they could claim a sovereign status?

Footprints

In pastoral communities, footprint tracks are used to find a lost animal, avoid an area of prey animals, or indicate recent activity of humans. The same tracks appear during the wintertime, when snow covers the ground, also in places like a desert, a forest, and a beach. Through those tracks, we can deduce and decide on what is best to keep on. With correspondence to human fingerprints on the internet, these fingerprints can pinpoint our identity, history, favorite activity, partners, even sometimes a chronic disease we suffer. Perhaps be used to predict future thoughts.




Benefits and cons of digital fingerprints

Indeed, digital fingerprints have huge benefits when used for the course of a community or society. For example: to improve the traffic of cities using a car GPS, to increase the capability of e-service providers like - essential government services using computer’s IP, etc.

“In 2015, at least 90% of Americans reported that privacy is “very important” to them, according to a survey by the Pew Research Center.” - mozilla.org

But because of the indirect relation nature between privacy and service improvement, digital fingerprints can be misused against the same community, perhaps specific individuals in the community. Furthermore, with the rise of the “Data-Dollar” age, tech firms will be keen to collect more of their user’s digital fingerprints to sell them to interested stakeholders. Worse than that, governments have used or can use digital fingerprints to spy on their people and are sometimes used to commit genocide if, God forbid, they fall on the bloody gloves.




Mitigation to reduce digital fingerprints

The following are common steps to reduce digital fingerprints:

  1. User awareness: as a generator of this datum, you should consolidate the necessity of the data you are generating. Is it vital to take your picture and post it on a particular platform? Is there an easier way you can socialize without compromising your privacy? Before you endorse an agreement, are you fully aware of its content?
  2. Legislation and government policies: one of the issues with tech firms is the different policies that comply regarding different geo-location. Why would Europe and the US have a safer policy than the rest of the world? What is the uniqueness of the Europeans and the Americans? Although technology is developing faster. Governments have to make a redoubled effort and hold out the pace of the technology by passing laws that ensure the rights and maximum privacy of their people.
