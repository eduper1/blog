Diversity

October 12, 2021 by Eduper

Look up in the sky, everything is in motion relatively to our planet's motion and our motion as we wander around above the Earth. Every object twinkles with its unique mixture of colors: blue, white, orange and red, excluding planets that glow: Venus and Jupiter.

From a distance, let observe our surrounding: our friends, neighbors, classmate, colleagues at work, our pets and objects around us are relatively in motion with us in unique reference of time. Everything, at that frame of time, around us are there for a reason wether revealed to us or not and vice versa.

Venus setting, it's the brightest of all in our observable sky though that may not be true. Glancing at the Northern stars, some are fainter while others are brighter. Some shine blue-white while others orange-red but all have one thing in common, they are not positioned where they seem to be. It is more likely we are perceiving it's position before hours ago.

Comparing to ourselves, we might have been introduced to ourselves since childhood as intelligent, beautiful, handsome, awesome, masters or misters though that may not be true. Perhaps others were raised in misfortune environment.

However, deep in ourselves how diverse are we? How much open are we to diversity? Surely, we can't scape-goat our perception of things for our failure to misjudge a book by its cover. More so, we need to be open minded about actual reality behind the scenes.

Look closer, stars are seem to be closer, aren't they? No, they are not closer to each other. They are thousands of light years away. Perhaps that perception is what makes us perceive dark night sky with twinkling stars near each other as beautiful and calming. It is true they all share the same stage. The sky. They all hold each other according to Sir Issac Newton.

Do we look alike? No need for us to look similar. Our thoughts, body-shapes, skin tone, economy class and religion should all drew us into one compound of many elements. Perhaps as a compound of different entities, we will redefine our relation with the environment, furthermore, our Universe

Summing up, diversity can't be reached with standardized system. A system that grades or degrades. Neither can we achieve it 100% but a continuous progress towards it. Just like the stars in the sky, we can all (with our differences) share and live in cohesion on this Earth.
