Diversity

October 12, 2021, by Eduper

Look up in the sky; everything is in motion relative to our planet's motion and our motion as we wander around above the Earth. Every object twinkles with its unique mixture of colors: blue, white, orange, and red, excluding planets that glow: Venus and Jupiter.

From a distance, let us observe our surroundings: our friends, neighbors, classmates, colleagues at work, our pets and objects around us are relatively in motion with us in unique reference to time. Everything, at that frame of time, around us is there for a reason, whether revealed to us or not, and vice versa.


Venus's setting, it's the brightest of all in our observable sky though that may not be true. Glancing at the Northern stars, some are fainter while others are brighter. Some shine blue-white while others orange-red but all have one thing in common, they are not positioned where they seem to be. It is more likely we are perceiving their positions before hours ago.

Compared to ourselves, we might have been introduced to ourselves since childhood as intelligent, beautiful, handsome, awesome, masters, or misters though that may not be true. Perhaps others were raised in a misfortune environment.

However, deep in ourselves how diverse are we? How much open are we to diversity? Surely, we can't scape-goat our perception of things for our failure to misjudge a book by its cover. More so, we need to be open-minded about the actual reality behind the scenes.

Look closer, stars are seen to be closer, aren't they? No, they are not closer to each other. They are apart thousands of light-years away. Perhaps that perception is what makes us perceive the night sky with twinkling stars near each other as beautiful and calming. They all indeed share the same stage. The sky. They all hold each other according to Sir Issac Newton.

Do we look alike? No need for us to look similar. Our thoughts, body shapes, skin tone, economic class, and religion should all draw us into one compound of many elements. Perhaps as a compound of different entities, we will redefine, one day, our relationship with the environment, furthermore, our Universe

Summing up, diversity can't be reached with a standardized system. A system that grades or degrades. Neither can we achieve it 100% but continuous progress towards it. Just like the stars in the sky, we can all (with our differences) share and live in cohesion on this Earth.
